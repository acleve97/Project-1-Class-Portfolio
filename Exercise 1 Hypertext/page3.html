Hydrangeas

History, Symbolism, Significance



In many cultures flowers come to have meanings. In Japanese culture hydrageas have become significant, and many believe the flower originated in Japan.
According to a Japanese legend, the flower became associated with heartful emotions, understanding, and apology. The legend tells a story about an emperor who once gave them to
the family of the girl whom he was in love with as an apology for neglecting her for business. Pink hydrangeas in particular are said to resemble the shape of a beating heart.

In other cultures and times however, the flower also had more negative associations. In the victorian era, many tied the flower to boastfulness, pride, and vanity.
This is because the hydragea produces little seeds, but many flowers. They were also given to individuals who had turned down a love interests
and this told the reciever that the gifter percieved them as a cold person.

Despite these also negative connotations, I find the Japanese legend and symbolism to be sweeter. Going back to Japanese importance,
the hydrangea's peak season is Japan's rainy season "tsuyu". One of the biggest symbols of the rainy season are hydrangeas.
During this time, Japan holds a plethora of festivals to celebrate the flower. Hydrangeas are also a popular decorative motif.
They make their appearance in Kimono, woodblock prints, paintings, tableware, jewelry, and other home decor accessories.
